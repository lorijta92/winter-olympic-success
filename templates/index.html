Introduction

This dashboard seeks to answer the question, “What is the relationship between a country’s wealth and Olympic performance?” To answer this, the team focused on the winter games in order to limit analysis. Future analyses would include the summer games.

Below are three interactive charts that look at different aspects to answer that question. The first, is a choropleth map to show the amount of gold medals won by each country out of the total amount of gold medals won by all nations. The second, is a bubble chart depicting the relationship between each country’s GDP per capital and the amount of medals won. Each marker’s color is representative of the medal type won, and its size representative of the country’s GDP. The third, is a line chart showing the relationship between population and the amount of medals won.

Percentage of Gold Medals Won

Hover over each country on the choropleth map to see the percentage of gold medals won by each each country relative to the total amount of gold medals available per year of the Winter Olympic Games.

GDP vs. Medals

See the relationship between GDP per capita and the amount of Olympic medals won for each country at the winter games from 1960 onward. The size of each marker is representative of each country's GDP that year.

Filter by medal type by clicking on the boxes above the chart.

Population vs. Medals

See how relative population size compares to percent of medals won from the 1960 winter games onward.
